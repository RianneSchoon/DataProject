Introduction

On the map, you can see the life expectancy of countries in the world. Choose men or women.
You can also choose to see the amount of doctors per 1000 inhabitants, as well as the amount of nurses, the available hospital beds, and the Gross Domestic Product.
The darker the color, the higher the value.
Slide the bar to select the time you want to see data of.
Click on a country to see the data for that country in the other graphs!

The line graph shows the amount of physicians, nurses, hospital beds, GDP and the life expectancy for a selected country over time.
You can select a line to update the scatterplot and see the relation between life expectancy and the other variables.

The scatterplot shows the influence on life expectancy of several variables (amount of doctors, nurses, hospital beds).
The size of the dots is the GDP.

Hover over countries on the map, lines, and dots to see additional information pop up!
Click checkboxes to select data.

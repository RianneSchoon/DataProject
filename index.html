Introduction

Do you want to live forever?
Most people do. But even if you avoid accidents and murderers, you still have to face
disease, infections, and eventually wear and tear due to old age - to eternity.
Nonetheless, we still try to prolong our lives as much as possible. The success of this endeavor varies greatly.

Let us explore some possible supporting means of health care and their influence on life expectancy around the world!

The map shows the life expectancy of countries in the world. You can also choose to see the amount
of doctors, nurses, available hospital beds, or the Gross Domestic Product. Darker colors mean higher values.
Slide the bar to select the year you want to see data of.
Click on a country to see its data in the other graphs!

The line graph shows the amount of care variables, GDP, and life expectancy for a selected country over time.
You can select a line to update the scatterplot and see the relation between variables.

Hover over countries on the map, lines, and dots to see additional information pop up!
Click checkboxes to select data.
